About the day

The goal isn't a side project — it's optimised development with AI.

What we're practising

Optimised development means picking the right AI tools for each step — planning, building, checking, shipping — and chaining them so the whole loop is faster than doing it the old way.

Developers already live in this world: Cursor agents, Claude Code, MCP servers, automations. This afternoon is when the rest of the team learns the same muscle — on a real task they own, with engineers and designers as co-pilots when code is involved.

What you leave with

One working thing you'd use on Monday — yes. But the real output is a repeatable workflow: which tool for which job, how you validated it, and what you'd keep using next week.

Mixed teams mean a marketer and a backend dev compare notes on the same stack. The cross-team connection happens because you're all learning to develop differently — not because we scheduled a networking break.

Hackathon rules

Internal event. AI stack. Validated output.

Roro Builder's Day is an internal company hackathon — for Roro people only. You bring a real task, build with the AI ecosystem in an optimised way, validate it on a live example, and demo both the result and the workflow.

01
Teams form at registration
Sign up with your teammates (2–4 people, ideally cross-functional). No separate team-formation block on the day — we start building right after kickoff.
02
Use the AI ecosystem
This hackathon is optimised development in practice — Claude Code, Cursor IDE, Cursor agents, MCP servers, Claude Projects, automations. Pick the right tool for each step; don't default to one chat window.
03
One task, one validated workflow
Each team picks one real weekly task and ships one working solution for it — plus the toolchain you'd actually reuse. Not a pitch deck.
04
Validate before you demo
Run it on a real example in the validation block. Fix what breaks. Your demo shows the output working live — not slides about what you might build.
05
Whoever feels the task builds it
Engineers and designers are co-pilots: they help people get unstuck with the stack, they don't take the keyboard. The task owner drives and demos.
06
Show your stack in the demo
Two minutes: the thing working, how you validated it, and which AI tools sped you up. We keep what lands on the keep-list — workflows, not just one-offs.

Quick questions

FAQ

The short version, before you bring your task.

What is optimised development?
Using the AI ecosystem — Claude Code, Cursor, agents, MCP, automations — with the right tool for each step, so you build faster and validate what you ship. The hackathon forces you to practise that loop, not just end up with a side project.
I don't code — can I still participate?
Yes. Optimised development isn't code-only — Claude Projects, automations, and agent-assisted workflows count. Engineers and designers co-pilot on the stack; the task owner still drives and demos.
Do I need to pick a category or team?
No. Generic ideas and your own task both count. Categories are optional prompts — the requirement is one real task, an AI toolchain, and a validated result.
How do teams work?
Form your team when you register — 2–4 people, ideally mixed across functions. Compare notes on the stack, not just the output. No separate team-formation time on the day.
What counts as "done"?
Working on one real example, validated in the session. Demo the output live, walk through how you used the AI tools, and show what you'd keep using next week.
What tools do I have to use?
Claude Code, Cursor IDE, Cursor agents, MCP servers, Claude Projects, and automations — pick what fits your task. The bar is an optimised stack, not a single generic chat.
Can I prep before the session?
Light prep is fine — gather docs, sketch the task, think about your toolchain. The build and validation happen in the session.
What happens after?
We note which workflows and tools land on the keep-list — the ones the team actually keeps using. Pressure to prove it's useful, not to polish a demo deck.
